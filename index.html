Exercises

Squats

A squat is a strength exercise in which the trainee lowers their hips from a standing position and then stands back up. During the descent, the hip and knee joints flex while the ankle joint dorsiflexes; conversely the hip and knee joints extend and the ankle joint plantarflexes when standing up. Squats also help the hip muscles.

DeadLift

The Deadlift is a hip-dominant movement that works the glutes, hamstrings, core, back, and trapezius muscle.
An individual can perform deadlifts with various barbells, weights, and repetition ranges. When executed with proper form, deadlifts are a safe and effective exercise for Building Muscle

Bench Press

The bench press, or chest press, is a weight training exercise where a person presses a weight upwards while lying horizontally on a weight training bench. Although the muscles primarily used are the pectoralis major, the anterior deltoids, and the triceps, among other stabilizing muscles.A pair of dumbbells can also be used

Lats Pull Down

The pull-down exercise is a strength training exercise designed to develop the latissimus dorsi muscle. It performs the functions of downward rotation and depression of the scapulae combined with adduction and extension of the shoulder joint.The cable lat pull-down is done where the handle is moved via a cable pulley, as opposed to doing pull-downs on a leverage machine.

Glutes Eexercise

Glute exercises are essential for building and strengthening the muscles in your backside. Strong glutes are important for overall health and fitness, as they help with posture, balance, and movement. They also play a role in reducing the risk of injuries, such as low back pain and knee pain. Some good Glutes Exercises : Squats, Lunges, Hip Thrust, Fire Hydrants, Kettlebell swings, etc.

Biceps Curl

The bicep curl mainly targets the biceps brachii, brachialis and brachioradialis muscles. The biceps is stronger at elbow flexion when the forearm is supinated and weaker when the forearm is pronated.The brachioradialis is at its most effective when the palms are facing inward, and the brachialis is unaffected by forearm rotation. The degree of forearm rotation affects the degree of muscle recruitment between the three muscles.
